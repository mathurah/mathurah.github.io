RBC Connect Intern, August 2017

In August 2017 I had the amazing opportunity to intern under RBC's technology and operations for the RBC Connect Team. RBC Connect is RBC's intranet/digital collaboration software. My manager gave me the chance to brainstorm problems about the software and develop a solution for it as my one month project.

The main projects I worked at RBC were:

  1. Redesigning the user interface of the help and support pages

  2. Developing an intiative to promote usage of the software to others

  3. Creating Tutorial Videos to help guide users through the software

And also blog about my experience there!

Redesigning the User Interface

As a new user to RBC Connect, I realized the software was quite daunting to use. RBC Connect has been recently established at RBC a few years ago and not all teams are using it for content publishing or project management. I decided to look into this and conducted user interviews with employees from several different teams -communications, developers, investing, and more.

I started asking several different "How Might We Questions" to decide on a problem to tackle during my internship term. My question became:

How might we make learning on Connect more efficient, user-friendly, and easy to navigate for new Community Managers?

Here are the before pictures of the site

It is clear to see that the information on these pages are overwhelming and hard to read. People are used to assosciating things with colours and images. Looking at the UI things such as your iPhone home screen or apps such as facebook, the UI is clean, simple, and easy to use. People can navigate to what they need in just a few clicks.

When it comes to onboarding, the new employee should not be overwlemed by all the material that is there. They should be able to clearly navigate to the information they need, efficiently and easily.

This training guide is labeled as training in a box, however it is just a large document full of links. THe typical user would most lkely skip reading important information such as "Community Goverance Requirements" because it links to an external document. They will also lose navigation from where they came rom.

From the user interviews I conducted, several people mentioned that it would be easier if this document had some sort of navigation where people can quickly find the information they need all in one place.

Redesigned pages

I created iconography for these pages and set up internal navigation for the site. There were a few limitations with RBC Connect -can't add css or javascript files to the site and more. However working with these limitatios allowed me to develop the skill of working with what I have, and not let it hinder my design capabilities. I also had to consider accessibility and ensured to add hover text elements so anyone would be able to interact with the site. I went with using colours from the rainbow -Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue etc... because people assosciate colours with objects, and this makes the user navigation easy and simple to use.

RBC Connect Contest

I realized that not a lot of people were using RBC Connect, so I wanted to start an initative to promote usage of this platform to employees of RBC. I, along with my fellow team member launched the #teamcollaboration contest, where each team would submit entries by posting how they use the platform for team collaboration and project management.

The contest was a success and we had over 30 teams enter from all across Canada. I developed the infographics, responded to the entries, regulated everything, and also translated the site to French to ensure everyone could enter.

Pairent

Pairent was a pitch I developed at a 6-hour hackathon. After volunteering at a new mother's charity, I realized that many new parents face barriers including lack of access for resources, some parents may have children who have disabilites and won't know how to deal with it, and also may not have emotional support or mentorship in this new journey.

Solution:Pairent is a platform that provides new parent(s) with mentorship, product reviews, a central resource hub of community centres/programs for new parents, a financial literacy component to help them save up for their kids' education, and a helpline for parents if they have any concerns.

H2Okay

Problem: Recently, native communities in Canada have had the issue of having contaminated water coming out of their taps. Unfortunately, this polluted water can be very dangerous to the community and can cause sickness to the residents. Testing the purity of water is not a procedure most households complete because it is pricey, time consuming, and water conditions can change overtime.

Solution: This project is a device that monitors the water conditons 24/7 to ensure safety. As water runs through the device it would reach a sector that will sense the pH level of the water. If it is too basic or acidic, then the data will be displayed on the sink and there is a system to notify your phone letting you know if your water is unsafe.

Shower Stream

Shower Stream is an app that makes showering with music more convenient and entertaining. The goal of this app is to help Canadian high school students establish a long-term habit of taking shorter showers, which would lower their energy footprint significantly because showers are 35% of a household’s energy consumption.

The core feature of Shower Stream is to encourage users to take shorter showers by playing music matching the length of the target shower time. In order for habits to be established, progress has to be shown. For that, in app's progress tab, users can see their calendar interface with streaks, money saved, energy saved, time saved and etc. To further motivate our users, the app gives user cool badges when certain milestone has been passed, which can be shared on social media. The badges are shown in the progress tab.

STCK

I was the Chief Technology Officer of STCK, which is a student run company through Junior Achievement Central Ontario. STCK aims to raise awareness and encourage youth to support homeless youth. We sell square stickers that clean your phone screen. The sticker can be placed on the back of your phone. Whenever you would like to use it, you peel it off the back and clean your screen.

In our society, there is a stigma that youth face; the stigma of being lazy and unproductive. There is a stigma that our generation, generation z, is the laziest one ever. Stck works to break that stigma. Our goal is to encourage fellow youth to go out into our communities and make an impact, whether that be through volunteering or community initiative. We had our own grassroots initiative of hosting volunteering events to give snack bags to the homeless. We achieved this with the support of several high school students in our community.
